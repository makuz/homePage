bio

Martin Kuzdowicz Computer programmer, music composer, guitar player and music producer. As I programmer I specialize in programming web applications in Java. In frameworks like Spring, Hibernate and WebObjects. More about my programming skills and career: https://www.linkedin.com/in/martinkuzdowicz As a musician I specialize in improvised music such as Jazz and modern Rock. I am/was in cooperation’s with polish bands like: Freeway Quintet , Soundrise , Flying Moses , Breslauer Cocktail. Selected achievements as a musician Audience Award at the International Getxo Jazz Festival in Spain 2013 (with Freeway Quintet band ) Second place on the Festival of Jazz Juniors in Krakow in 2010 (with Freeway Quintet band ) Grand Prix - National Competition Jazz RCK pro Jazz Festival 2010 in Kolobrzeg (with Freeway Quintet band ) TVP KULTURA Award (Polish television) - Festival NIEMEN NON STOP 2009 Slupsk (with Soundrise band ) Composing music for the silent film The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari for the 100th anniversary of the Technical Universities in Wroclaw in 2010 (with Freeway Quintet band ) Composing music for the play " Dziewictwo " theater Narybek From Poland during the Festival FAMA 2009 Swinoujscie (with Freeway Quintet band ) Andrew Jakóbiec Prize for the most talented creators of jazz at festival FAMA 2009 Swinoujscie (with Freeway Quintet band ) Third place in the II Student Instrumental Competition "Fryderyk Chopin" Zielona Gora 2006 in the category of jazz , for the jazz interpretation of Waltz cis- moll op. 67 No. 2 by Frederic Chopin Currently my fields of interest are focused on how can I combine my music skills and my programming skills. To develop this interests I am using now among others the Sonic PI environment. Sonic PI is something like a programming language environment for creating music. As I go further into programming I begin to see more and more similarities between programming and composing music. This similarities I see for example in comparing design patterns and music forms or list of objects comparing to scales in music. As for my current interest in programming I am excited now about big data and machine learning concepts.
